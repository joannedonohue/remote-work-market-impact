# Remote Work Impact on U.S. Commercial Real Estate & Transit Markets

Analysis of how the shift to remote work reshaped office vacancy rates, commercial real estate pricing, and public transit ridership across 24 major U.S. cities using BLS employment data and real estate market indicators.

---

## Key Findings

- **Office vacancy rates** increased significantly in tech-heavy metros (Bay Area, Austin, Denver) vs. more modest shifts in finance hubs (Manhattan, Chicago)
- **Rent decline** observed in cities with high pre-pandemic office concentration; Sun Belt cities showed relative resilience
- **Transit ridership** correlated strongly with return-to-office rates — cities with mandated RTO policies (Dallas, Miami) recovered faster
- **City-specific outcomes** varied widely, driven by industry mix, housing costs, and local policy

## Methodology

1. Sourced BLS remote work and employment data across metro areas (2019–2023)
2. Paired with commercial real estate vacancy and pricing data per city
3. Analyzed transit ridership recovery as a proxy for physical office attendance
4. Identified clusters of cities by recovery trajectory and structural resilience
